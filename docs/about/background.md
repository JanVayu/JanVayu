# Project Background

## Origin

JanVayu began as a civic response to the repeated failure of annual pollution "seasons" in North India to produce lasting policy accountability. Every winter, Delhi's air quality collapses. Every spring, the crisis recedes from public memory. The cycle repeats.

The project was created on the conviction that **public memory is a prerequisite for public accountability**. If the data, the court orders, the policy failures, and the human stories are archived and made permanently accessible, they cannot be dismissed or forgotten.

---

## Name

**JanVayu** (जनवायु) translates as "People's Air" — a compound of *jan* (जन, people/public) and *vayu* (वायु, air/wind). The name was chosen in January 2026 following community input, replacing the earlier name "Vayu Smriti" (वायु स्मृति — Air Memory). The new name was selected for better linguistic inclusivity across India's diverse language communities.

In Tamil: ஜன்வாயு | In Bengali: জনবায়ু | In Marathi: जनवायु

---

## Scope

JanVayu is **national in scope** — it covers 30+ cities across all major regions of India, not only Delhi-NCR. It also specifically highlights disparities:

- Women disproportionately exposed to household air pollution
- Informal workers without protection from outdoor exposure
- Dalit and Adivasi communities near industrial zones
- Children whose developing lungs are most vulnerable

---

## Governance

JanVayu is:

- **Non-partisan** — not affiliated with any political party, government body, or corporate entity
- **Independently maintained** — decisions are guided by editorial principles, not donor requirements
- **Open source** — code is MIT-licensed; content is CC BY-NC-SA 4.0

Editorial principles:
1. Factual accuracy and source transparency
2. Respect for affected communities
3. Non-partisanship — criticise policies and systems, not parties or individuals
4. Accessibility across languages and literacy levels
