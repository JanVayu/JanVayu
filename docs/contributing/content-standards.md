# Content Standards

All content on JanVayu must meet these standards before it is added to the platform.

---

## What We Accept

| ✅ Accepted | ❌ Not Accepted |
|------------|----------------|
| Verified data from official sources (CPCB, WHO, Lancet, etc.) | Unverified claims or rumours |
| Peer-reviewed research papers | Partisan political content |
| Credible journalism from established outlets | Commercial promotions or sponsored content |
| Firsthand citizen testimonies (with consent) | Content that violates privacy without consent |
| Official policy documents and court orders | Misinformation or pseudoscience |
| Accurate translations of verified content | Translations of unverified content |

---

## Source Verification

Every data point must have a primary source. When contributing content:

1. **Link to the original source** — not a news article summarising a study, but the study itself
2. **Note the date** — especially for statistics (pollution data changes; always cite the year)
3. **Note the methodology** — for health/mortality estimates, which model was used matters

### Acceptable Primary Sources

- Government: CPCB, MoEFCC, State PCBs, CAQM, PRANA, Ministry publications
- International: WHO, World Bank, UN agencies
- Academic: Peer-reviewed journals (Lancet, PNAS, NEJM, Nature, etc.)
- Legal: Indian Kanoon, NGT/Supreme Court order archives
- Investigative: CREA, CSE, UrbanEmissions.info, established newspapers

### Sources Requiring Caution

- Social media posts (use for citizen voices section only, with clear labelling)
- Press releases (cross-check with underlying data)
- Aggregator websites (always go to the original study)

---

## Non-Partisan Standard

JanVayu is non-partisan. Content must:

- Criticise policies and institutional failures — not individuals or political parties
- Apply the same standard of scrutiny to all governments (central, state, regardless of party)
- Present data, not opinions
- Attribute actions to institutions, not politicians

---

## Language and Tone

- Use plain English accessible to an educated general audience
- Avoid jargon without explanation (e.g., always explain PM2.5 before using the abbreviation)
- Lead with data; support with context
- Translations should capture meaning accurately — do not oversimplify

---

## Privacy and Consent

- Citizen testimonies must be anonymised unless the contributor has explicitly consented to be named
- Do not publish personally identifiable information from RTI responses
- Photos of individuals require their consent
