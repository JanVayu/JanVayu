# IQAir 2025: India's Air Got Worse

**Published:** 12 April 2026

---

IQAir released its [8th annual World Air Quality Report](https://www.iqair.com/world-air-quality-report) on March 24, 2026, and the findings for India are sobering.

## Key Findings

**Loni is now the most polluted city on Earth.** The city in Uttar Pradesh recorded an annual average PM2.5 of **112.5 ug/m3** — a 23% increase from 2024 and more than **22 times** the WHO guideline of 5 ug/m3. It replaces Byrnihat, Meghalaya, which held the unwanted title last year.

**Global compliance is dropping.** Only **14% of cities** worldwide met the WHO annual PM2.5 guideline, down from 17% the previous year. Air quality progress is stalling as wildfire smoke and climate change intensify pollution globally.

**India's national average remains extreme.** At **48.9 ug/m3**, India's average PM2.5 is nearly 10 times the WHO recommended limit. The five most polluted countries globally — Pakistan, Bangladesh, Tajikistan, Chad, and DRC — are all neighbours or share similar structural challenges.

## What Changed

Two developments stand out:

1. **US State Department ended embassy monitoring (March 2025).** Millions of people in 44 countries lost access to independent air quality data. For India, this matters because embassy monitors often provided a check on official readings. CREA reported that six countries were left with no monitoring at all.

2. **New causal evidence on mortality.** Two studies in *The Lancet Planetary Health* established causal (not just correlational) links between PM2.5 and death in India — finding **1.5 million additional deaths per year** compared to WHO-guideline conditions. A *Science Advances* study showed that whatever air quality improvements India has achieved have been deeply unequal: wealthier urban areas improve while poorer regions stagnate.
