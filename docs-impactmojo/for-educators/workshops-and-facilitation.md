# Workshops & Facilitation

This guide helps educators and trainers design and deliver workshops using ImpactMojo's curriculum.

---

## Planning Framework

Follow these six steps:

1. **Establish learning objectives** — what should participants know or be able to do afterward?
2. **Select a content track** — choose from MEL & Research, Economics & Policy, Gender & Equity, Governance & Society, Health & Wellbeing, or Communication & Data
3. **Pick 2–4 relevant modules** — mix foundational and flagship content
4. **Incorporate a game** — choose from 16 interactive simulations
5. **Add a lab component** — select from 19 hands-on workbenches
6. **Prepare handouts** — download from 400+ single-page references

## Facilitation Best Practices

### Games

> **The debrief is more important than the game itself.**

- Introduce games without theoretical explanation — let participants discover concepts experientially
- Allow 20–30 minutes for gameplay, 15–20 minutes for structured debrief
- Connect game outcomes to course content in the debrief

### Handouts

Handouts serve three purposes:
1. **Pre-workshop** — assigned reading to level-set participants
2. **In-session** — worksheets and reference cards during activities
3. **Take-home** — reference materials for continued learning

### Mixed-Ability Groups

- Start with foundational courses to build shared vocabulary
- Use ImpactLex for terminology support
- Pair experienced practitioners with newer learners during labs
- Provide multilingual handouts where available

## Cohort-Based Learning

For extended training programmes, use the cohort feature:
- Organisation admins create scheduled training groups
- Set enrollment windows and deadlines
- Track group progress on the team dashboard
- Enable discussion threads for asynchronous collaboration
