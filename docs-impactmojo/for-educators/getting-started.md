# Getting Started

ImpactMojo requires no login, no payment, and no software installation. Visit [www.impactmojo.in](https://www.impactmojo.in) and start learning immediately.

---

## Step 1 — Explore Without an Account

All core content is accessible without registration:
- 48 courses (9 flagship + 39 foundational)
- 16 interactive games
- 19 labs
- 400+ handouts
- 200 case studies
- 500+ curated papers
- 247 datasets in the Dataverse

---

## Step 2 — Create a Free Account (Optional)

Registering unlocks:
- **Progress tracking** — the platform remembers where you left off
- **Bookmarks** — save resources for later
- **Personal notes** — capture reflections while reading modules
- **Learning streak** — track consecutive study days
- **Community access** — participate in discussions

---

## Step 3 — Choose a Learning Track

Pick a track that matches your role or interest:

| Track | Best For |
|-------|---------|
| MEL & Research | M&E officers, researchers, programme evaluators |
| Economics & Policy | Policy analysts, economists, advocacy teams |
| Gender & Equity | Gender specialists, inclusion programme staff |
| Governance & Society | Governance researchers, civic engagement practitioners |
| Health & Wellbeing | Public health workers, nutrition programme staff |
| Communication & Data | Data analysts, communications officers |

---

## Step 4 — Build a Learning Path

Recommended progression:

1. **Start with a Foundational Course** — get the basics in 30–60 minutes
2. **Move to a Flagship Course** — deep-dive with ~13 modules, case studies, and lexicons
3. **Reinforce with a Game** — 20–30 minutes of play + 15–20 minutes of debrief
4. **Apply in a Lab** — hands-on workbench producing a tangible output
5. **Reference Handouts** — single-page cheat sheets for quick recall

---

## Step 5 — Search and Discover

Use **Ctrl+K** (or **Cmd+K** on Mac) to search across:
- Courses
- Case studies
- Papers (DevDiscourses)
- Glossary terms (ImpactLex)

---

## For Workshop Leaders

If you're planning to use ImpactMojo in a training session:
1. Assign pre-reading from foundational courses
2. Run a simulation game (20–30 min play + 15–20 min debrief)
3. Use case studies as group discussion prompts
4. Distribute handouts as take-home references

See [Workshops & Facilitation](workshops-and-facilitation.md) for detailed session plans.
