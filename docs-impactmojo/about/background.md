# Project Background

ImpactMojo was created to make development economics and policy education accessible to practitioners, researchers, NGO workers, and students across South Asia.

---

## The Problem

Development professionals in South Asia often lack access to structured, high-quality training in monitoring & evaluation, research methods, economics, and policy analysis. Existing resources are either:
- **Expensive** — professional courses cost hundreds of dollars
- **Inaccessible** — require fast internet, modern devices, or specific software
- **Western-centric** — case studies and examples from North America and Europe
- **Fragmented** — no single platform covers the breadth of skills practitioners need

---

## The Solution

ImpactMojo is a free, comprehensive learning platform purpose-built for South Asian development professionals. It provides:

- **48 courses** covering MEL, research methods, economics, policy, gender, governance, health, and communication
- **16 interactive games** — economic simulations for experiential learning
- **19 labs** — hands-on workbenches producing real outputs
- **400+ handouts** — printable single-page reference materials
- **200 case studies** from 117 countries with South Asian focus
- **500+ curated papers** selected for practitioner relevance
- **247 datasets** in the Dataverse from World Bank, DHS, NFHS, NSSO

All of this works on slow connections, older devices, and offline via PWA — because that's the reality for many practitioners in the field.

---

## Design Philosophy

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Free at the point of use | No paywall for core content |
| No build step | Static HTML/CSS/JS — runs anywhere |
| Offline-first | Progressive Web App with content caching |
| South Asia-focused | Indian folk art illustrations, local case studies, regional data |
| Practitioner-first | Skills and tools people use in their daily work |
| Multilingual | 6 languages — English, Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, Telugu, Marathi |

---

## Sustainability

Premium tiers (Practitioner, Professional, Organisation) fund ongoing development. The platform uses manual UPI payment to eliminate gateway fees for Indian users.

---

## Relationship to JanVayu

ImpactMojo and [JanVayu](https://www.janvayu.in) are sister projects under the same team. JanVayu focuses on air quality accountability; ImpactMojo focuses on development education. Both share the same commitment to open access, South Asian relevance, and practitioner utility.
